My favorite artists

Arts living with me

In November 2015, I bought my first painting in 25 years. It was "Golden Eagle" by the picture book artist Ms. Machiko MiroKo. I thought that is the first time I have bought an original painting that is not prints nor silk screens. I realized once again that it is very comfortable to live with the arts I love. Since then, I have been bought several original paintings by young artists every year from galleries or crowdfunding.

I'll say again. It is very comfortable to live with the arts you love.

Sayaka Imai

I came across her works at Xmas Art Festa in Ginza, Tokyo. It was December 2016. At that time, I thought they were three-dimensional paintings, and I was looking at them from various angles. So I was so surprised when Ms. Sako, a gallerist at Seizan Gallery, taught me that they were papercuts.

Tomoyuki Kambe

I came across his works at the Xmas Art Festa in Ginza, December 2016. His paintings were expensive for me, that time. I was not sure whether to buy one or not, when I returned after visiting another gallery, I found his works have sold out. I was so disappointed. I could not see his work since, because he had been working on a big project.

I met his works again at Art Fair Tokyo 2019. I finally welcomed his work into my home, then.

Kanako Ozawa

I came across her works at Art Fair Tokyo 2019 in March 2019. At that time, her paintings which I liked have already sold, so I decided to wait for the fair next year. However, due to the coronavirus, Art Fair Tokyo 2020 was canceled.

I bought one of her paintings at Art Fair Tokyo 2021. I was finally able to escort her painting to my home.
